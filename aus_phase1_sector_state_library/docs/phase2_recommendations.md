# Phase 2 recommendation memo

## Bottom-line judgement

**Yes** — this Phase 1 library is sufficient for a **first reduced-form national model** whose primary near-term purpose is to explore the interaction between whole-economy decarbonisation and the electricity sector.

It is strongest in sectors where Australia already has good aggregate evidence and clear technology-direction signals. It is weaker where process-chain detail is inherently important.

## Where the Phase 1 library is strongest

1. **Electricity supply**  
   Strong enough now for a first national reduced-form model.

2. **Buildings**  
   Residential and commercial service bundles are defensible for national reduced-form work.

3. **Road transport**  
   Passenger road is reasonably strong; freight road is usable with caveats.

4. **Cement**  
   One of the best-supported hard-to-abate sectors in the package.

## Where the Phase 1 library is weakest

1. **Generic industrial heat**  
   Useful for MVP fuel-switch sensitivity, but weak as an explanatory industrial representation.

2. **Steel**  
   Valuable to include, but still strongly assumption-driven.

3. **Agriculture**  
   Credible only as a coarse residual emissions-and-energy block.

4. **Engineered removals**  
   DACCS remains exploratory and should not anchor core runs.

## Direct answers to the brief's open questions

### 1. Which Phase 1 sectors can be represented credibly using reduced-form sector states with strong evidence?

Most credibly:

- electricity supply,
- residential buildings,
- commercial buildings,
- passenger road transport,
- cement.

Reasonably credibly, but with more caveats:

- freight road transport,
- coarse land sequestration.

### 2. Which sectors are only weakly supportable and should remain coarse initially?

- generic industrial heat,
- agriculture,
- removals (especially DACCS),
- steel beyond the basic route archetypes.

### 3. Which sectors most urgently require explicit process-chain expansion in Phase 2?

Priority order:

1. **Steel**
2. **Industrial subsectors currently hidden inside generic industrial heat**
3. **Cement**, especially if the model needs explicit clinker / SCM / CCS competition
4. **Agriculture**, if the model will rely on agricultural mitigation or land competition materially

### 4. For electricity, is a single national frontier sufficient, or is regional differentiation necessary even in Phase 1?

A **single national frontier is sufficient** for Phase 1 if the objective is a fast national reduced-form model.

However, the first electricity upgrade in Phase 2 should be at least:

- NEM versus non-NEM, or
- a simple state-level split.

Regional differentiation becomes important quickly if the model starts to care about:

- region-specific electrification load growth,
- hydrogen production location,
- non-NEM mining and industry loads,
- or transmission bottlenecks.

### 5. For buildings and transport, what service definitions are most defensible for national reduced-form use?

Most defensible service definitions:

- buildings: **baseline-normalised service bundles** split into residential and commercial
- passenger road: **passenger-km**
- freight road: **tonne-km**

These definitions are clear, calibration-friendly, and map well to later technology splits.

### 6. For generic industrial heat, how much explanatory power is lost relative to a more process-specific industrial representation?

A **material amount** of explanatory power is lost.

The generic heat representation is still useful for:

- commodity demand shifts,
- broad gas-versus-electricity sensitivity,
- rough industrial abatement envelopes.

But it is weak for:

- subsector-specific abatement ranking,
- retrofit realism,
- process integration effects,
- and high-confidence fuel-switch feasibility.

### 7. For steel and cement, what are the most defensible small sets of representative sector states?

**Steel**
- conventional BF-BOF
- CCS-influenced BF-BOF
- scrap EAF
- hydrogen DRI-electric route

**Cement**
- conventional cement
- low-clinker / alternative-fuels cement
- deep-abatement cement with CCS

Those sets are compact but still span the most important reduced-form trade-offs.

### 8. For agriculture and removals, what is credible now and what should be deferred?

Credible now:
- coarse agriculture bundles for national energy and emissions accounting,
- cautious land-sequestration supply state.

Should be deferred from core decision weight:
- detailed agricultural technology competition,
- land-use competition,
- DACCS in any strong normative role.

## Recommended Phase 2 work program

### Highest priority

1. **Steel process-chain build**
2. **Industrial sector split-out from generic industrial heat**
3. **Electricity regionalisation**
4. **Cement process-chain refinement if industrial process detail becomes central**

### Second priority

5. **Agriculture and land-use refinement**
6. **Explicit sequestration / CCS infrastructure representation**
7. **Stock turnover in buildings and transport**

## Recommendation on using this Phase 1 package in practice

Use this package now for:

- MVP reduced-form scenario analysis,
- national commodity-demand trajectories,
- emissions trajectory testing,
- rapid whole-economy / electricity interaction work.

But keep the following sectors under tighter review:

- generic industrial heat,
- steel,
- agriculture mitigation,
- removals.

## Final recommendation

The package is **good enough to proceed** as a Phase 1 national reduced-form library.

It should not be mistaken for the endpoint. Its real value is that it creates a reviewable and traceable base from which the next explicit-process upgrades can be chosen rationally rather than guessed.
